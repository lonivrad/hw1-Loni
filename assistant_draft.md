Title:

Fixing How People Make Decisions in Aging Care

Introduction:

I'm an ER nurse and MBA candidate at Johns Hopkins Carey Business School. I also study computer science at University of Pennsylvania. I spend most of my time at the intersection of healthcare and technology, looking at where things break in real life and how to turn those gaps into practical systems that actually help people.

Problem Area:

The aging care system does not have a service problem. It has a decision problem. Families are forced to make serious choices with almost no clear or usable information. At the same time, caregivers and operators deal with constant uncertainty around what is safe, what is allowed, and what to do next. I care about building tools that take messy, scattered information and turn it into something people can actually use to make confident decisions.

Where Generative AI Fits:
- Turn inspection reports and care data into clear and simple comparisons that families can understand
- Help caregivers know what to do in real situations instead of guessing or overcalling for help
- Take care needs or home issues and turn them into clear next steps with the right people to act on them
