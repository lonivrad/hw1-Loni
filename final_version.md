Title:
Fixing Decision-Making in Aging Care

Introduction:
I’m an ER nurse and MBA candidate at Johns Hopkins Carey Business School, and I study computer science at University of Pennsylvania. Working in the ER, I repeatedly see older patients come in not because of a true medical emergency, but because something simple broke down at home-missed medications, no transportation, or lack of basic support. That gap between everyday needs and clinical care is where I focus, building systems that actually work outside of ideal conditions.

Problem Area:
The aging care system does not have a service shortage, it has a decision failure. Families are forced to make high-stakes choices with fragmented, hard-to-interpret information, often under time pressure after a hospital visit. Meanwhile, caregivers and small operators navigate unclear expectations, inconsistent regulations, and constant second-guessing about what is appropriate or safe. I’m focused on building tools that translate scattered data and real-world constraints into clear, usable decisions people can act on immediately.

Where Generative AI Fits:
	•	Convert inspection reports, incident logs, and care data into structured, comparable insights families can actually evaluate
	•	Support caregivers in real-time with situation-based guidance grounded in common scenarios, not generic instructions
	•	Translate vague care needs or home breakdowns into specific next steps, matched with the right level of support
